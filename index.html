I’m Fei, a Principal Scientist at Lila Sciences, where I use machine learning to design proteins, mRNA, and DNA regulatory elements for therapeutics.

I earned my PhD in Physics from the Niels Bohr Institute at the University of Copenhagen, developing computational methods to study protein structure and dynamics. My work combined dynamic Bayesian networks and Monte Carlo simulations to model protein dynamics [Ref 1]. I also accurately predicted the structure of an amyloid protein by decoding evolutionary patterns in protein sequences [Ref 2], 5 years before AlphaFold’s release.

During my postdoc with Robert Best at the NIH, we discovered that generative models capturing covariant patterns within a protein family can be used effectively to design novel protein sequences. We published (arguably) the first study applying a generative model to create high-diversity protein sequences, validated through wet-lab experiments [Ref 3], followed by a subsequent study on a larger protein [Ref 4]. In addition to protein design, I also developed molecular simulation methods that explain how proteins fold on the ribosome [Ref 5–8].

Afterwards, at Novoyzmes A/S, I applied my research and designed several enzymes products for the global market, including Pristine®, Carezyme® and Mannaway® etc.
