Here's a little background about myself.
After graduating with a degree in Mechanical Engineering from Carnegie Mellon University, I worked as an assistant product manager at a BEA sensors for a year. I helped manage a few door sensor products but I never felt fulfilled in my work.

This was when I started thinking back to my Carnegie Mellon days where I did really well in an advanced level C++ course and was then asked to be the TA for the next semester. I remembered how engaging, challenging, and satisfying coding was and regretted not choosing software as my career path! During my last three months at work, I built webapps using Ruby on Rails and restudied data structures/algorithms in python.

Outside of coding, I like attending church activities, spending time with my family, and going to the gym. I have also self-taught myself how to play guitar by ear, and am a self-taught piano player by ear as well.
