In total I played 58 games for the first time over the period. (16 of them was children games (I'll write another post on that later), so 42 games qualify as nominees.) As last year I go from light to... meatier... categories. But, this year, I have tried to select only 9 games. For simplicity. And it allows me to do this:



Off we go.

Filler games

Kingdomino worked quite well with a six years old. Slightly too complicated scoring, but interesting to build cute landscapes with monsters et co. #bgg #j2s #boardgames #kingdomino #brunocathala #juegodemesa #gamingwithkids #games #cyrilbouquet
A photo posted by Of Cardboard Sheep (@do_meeples_dream) on Dec 10, 2016 at 3:38am PST

Kingdomino is one of Bruno Cathala's many new games of 2016. An excellent take on, well, domino. (Kind of.) This really clever 15 minutes filler is along with Deep Sea Adventure my choices of the year for this category. I had the latter on the radar before Essen Spiel this year because of it's amazing minimalist artwork and simple push your luck gameplay. This lived up to expectations and is one of my most played games from the loot of 2016. It has a fairly claustrophobic theme, so I guess it is not for everyone...
Deep Sea Adventure by Oink games. Great push your luck undersea treasure hunt... simulator... #deepseaadventure #oinkgames #spiel16 #bgg
A photo posted by Of Cardboard Sheep (@do_meeples_dream) on Oct 14, 2016 at 8:58am PDT

Party game

Codenames: Pictures, however, probably is for everyone. This visually oriented game manages to improve the hit formula from 2015's word based Codenames. Since it is image based it works better (but not perfect) in settings where players don't have the same mother tongue...
Got to play some tense rounds of 6-player Codenames: Pictures yesterday. Most excellent. #codenamespictures #bgg #jeux #boardgames #boardgamegeek #infrench
A photo posted by Of Cardboard Sheep (@do_meeples_dream) on Dec 1, 2016 at 11:10pm PST

Gateway games

Restarting Fabled Fruit with another group of players. (Saved the previous game, obviously.) Fascinating stuff! #fabledfruit #2f #friedemannfriese #bgg #jeux #boardgames
A photo posted by Of Cardboard Sheep (@do_meeples_dream) on Oct 24, 2016 at 3:39pm PDT

Fabled Fruit and Evolution: Climate share this victory in this category. The former for its simplicity and elegant ever evolving game play trough Friese's take on the legacy game concept, and the latter for its simple rules and tons of variability with the ever evolving species "naturally selected" by the players.
Another session of the amazing Evolution Climate with @houseofcardboards and @abhoth_ . I really like how well theme and mechanics mesh in this game -- leading to some fascinating stories of survival, food, cannibalism, extinction etc #evolutionclimate #bgg #jeux #boardgames #kickstarter
A photo posted by Of Cardboard Sheep (@do_meeples_dream) on Nov 15, 2016 at 11:20am PST

Solo game

A little Tiny Epic Galaxies for two. This is my favourite Tiny Epic game -- so far. The solitaire variant is also great fun! #bgg #tinyepicgalaxies #jeux #boardgames #j2s #spiel #game #scottalmes #gamelyngames #repost @mortenjohs
A photo posted by Of Cardboard Sheep (@do_meeples_dream) on Dec 29, 2016 at 3:03am PST

Tiny Epic Galaxies is by far the game I have played the most solitaire last year. It's great fun to try to beat the, rather large, selection of "AIs". Especially with my 6 year old as a sidekick.

Ahead of the excellent Inis. 
The Castles of Burgundy: The Card Game comes in as number 3. I haven't played the "original", but I love this card variant, and, as an added bonus, the solitaire rules are solid.

Honourable mentions

Mombasa from Pfister started... #mombasa #spiel16 #bgg #jeux
A photo posted by Of Cardboard Sheep (@do_meeples_dream) on Oct 16, 2016 at 4:41am PDT

Mombasa, ah, Mombasa. I just got to play about one third of it at the last day of the Essen Spiel, so I can't really put it properly on this list... Amazing Pfister game! (although I am, for once, a bit put off by the theme.) 
Isle of Skye: From Chieftain to King is another Pfister game I got to play in Essen. Kind of. We improvised a six player variant with only new players, so it was far from ideal. Looking very much forward to play this again.
I have only played a kind of solitaire teach-myself-the-rules variant of Phil Eklund's Bios: Genesis, but I can't wait to play the whole thing.
My first bacteria! #biosgenesis #bgg #jeux #boardgames #phileklund #bacteria #life #biosgenesis
A photo posted by Of Cardboard Sheep (@do_meeples_dream) on Nov 29, 2016 at 12:18pm PST

The game I look forward to the most in 2017

The Great Western Trail Voila. This game looks like it could be Pfister's best so far. I can't wait.
